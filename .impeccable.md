## Design Context

### Users
Bank executives (CTO, VP Engineering, Board members) and engineering/ops leads at a large Indonesian bank. They use this dashboard at their desks in well-lit offices during work hours — checking portfolio health before meetings, reviewing status during weekly syncs, or preparing board reports. They need clarity and confidence, not raw data density.

### Brand Personality
**Calm, precise, authoritative** — like a premium private banking report. The interface should make users feel informed and in control, never overwhelmed or alarmed. Trust is paramount; this is a tool that influences multi-billion Rupiah decisions.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Refined / luxury** — subtle, premium feel, elegant restraint. Think private banking interfaces, not SaaS dashboards. The premium feel comes from what you leave out, not what you add.

- **Theme**: Light (well-lit office context, projector-friendly for boardrooms)
- **References**: Bloomberg Terminal's information density done right; Swiss financial reports; Stripe's dashboard clarity
- **Anti-references**: Generic Grafana/Datadog clones, anything with neon accents on dark backgrounds, gradient text, colored border stripes
- **Language**: Fully English (with standard tech terms: ROI, MAU, UX)

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over density** — Every screen answers one question clearly before revealing detail
2. **Earned attention** — Color and emphasis are budgeted. Red means something has gone wrong, not "this is a header"
3. **Quiet confidence** — The interface feels like it was designed by a team that knows banking, not generated by a tool
4. **Progressive narrative** — Lead with insight ("2 apps need attention"), not raw metrics ("12 apps total")
5. **Restraint is luxury** — The premium feel comes from what you leave out, not what you add
